## ASSIGNMENT

Loss Solutions Group was contacted by Mr. Brad Vifquain of Hanover Insurance. The scope of this assignment was to address the evaporator water coil for a Trane packaged rooftop air conditioning unit located at Pasteur Plaza Surgery Center, 7909 Fredericksburg Rd Ste 222, San Antonio, TX 78229. The unit reportedly sustained damage as a result of cold weather/freeze-up conditions on or about January 26, 2026, during Winter Storm Fern.

## FINDINGS AND ANALYSIS

Loss Solutions Group contacted the insured, Ms. Laura Rodrick of Pasteur Plaza Surgery Center, at (210) 614-4544 x1991. Ms. Rodrick reported that it got very cold in the San Antonio area and the rooftop unit stopped working. She stated that Trane came out to the location and found that the evaporator water coil had burst. She noted that the unit is large and a crane would be needed for the replacement. She further reported that the unit has been repaired and is back up and running. Ms. Rodrick estimated the unit to be from approximately 2001.

Loss Solutions Group contacted the vendor, Mr. Chris Ust, Account Manager with Trane US Inc., at (726) 205-9127. Mr. Ust confirmed the scope of the repairs and provided a breakdown of costs. He reported that the work involved four technicians over two full days, with crane support for both days. The scope included the custom-made evaporator water coil, welding, dielectric fittings, and various other materials needed to complete the installation. Mr. Ust confirmed the coil specifications and described it as having copper tubes with aluminum fins and a galvanized steel casing. He also confirmed the applicable sales tax rate of 8.25%.

Loss Solutions Group contacted Coil Replacement Company at (866) 297-0558. Mr. Adrian Kniahynycky, Sales Engineer, provided a quote for a custom-built chilled water coil matching the specifications of the damaged unit.

A total amount of $32,998.54 (excluding applicable sales tax) was presented.

## SCOPE OF DAMAGE

On or about January 26, 2026, the evaporator water coil within a Trane packaged rooftop air conditioning unit (Model: TSCA012U0A, Serial: K01L59818C) at Pasteur Plaza Surgery Center reportedly sustained damage as a result of cold weather/freeze-up conditions during Winter Storm Fern. Photos provided show burst copper U-bends on the chilled water coil.

## AGE & CONDITION

Based on information gathered during the investigation, the Trane packaged rooftop air conditioning unit was manufactured in approximately 2001, making it roughly twenty-five (25) years old at the time of loss. The equipment nameplate information had faded due to prolonged sun exposure. Based on its age, the unit was in average condition at the time of loss.

## CAUSE OF LOSS

The evaporator water coil reportedly sustained damage as a result of cold weather/freeze-up conditions during the 2026 Winter Storm Fern event on or about January 26, 2026. The freezing temperatures caused the water within the coil to expand, resulting in burst copper tubing. Photos provided support the reported cause of loss, showing split and burst copper U-bends consistent with freeze damage.

## REPARABILITY

Due to the nature and extent of the damage to the evaporator water coil, replacement of the coil was deemed necessary. The coil was not a candidate for repair, as the burst copper tubing throughout the coil rendered it beyond economical repair. The unit has since been repaired with a new evaporator water coil installed by Trane US Inc.
