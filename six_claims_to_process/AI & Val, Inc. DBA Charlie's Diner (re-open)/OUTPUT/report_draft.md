**Assignment:**

Loss Solutions Group was contacted by Mr. John Leonard of Travelers Insurance. The scope of this assignment was to review and validate an invoice/estimate in the amount of $31,853.30 for Ordinance or Law/Code Upgrade electrical repairs reportedly required to bring the insured's restaurant into compliance for re-opening following a reported fire event on August 13, 2024.

**Findings and Analysis:**

Loss Solutions Group contacted the insured, Mr. Michael Alfano with Al & Val, Inc., DBA Charlie's Diner (413-313-1007), to discuss the additional code upgrade work. During our conversation, Mr. Alfano explained that following the fire, the building was considered a total loss. As a result of the fire and subsequent rebuilding, the local fire marshal and building inspector required that the entire electrical and fire alarm system be brought up to current code before the restaurant could re-open. Mr. Alfano reported that none of this equipment existed in the diner previously, as the building had been grandfathered under older codes. Once the fire occurred and repairs were needed, the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) required full compliance with current codes. Mr. Alfano reported that the work has been completed and the building has been inspected and approved for occupancy.

Loss Solutions Group contacted the insured's electrical vendor, Alfano Electric LLC (413-636-3676), and spoke with Mr. Tony Alfano to discuss the code upgrade work. Mr. Tony Alfano confirmed that the proposal covers only code-required upgrades. He explained that the entire diner's wiring was destroyed in the fire, and every wire had to be pulled out due to the destruction. The work performed was strictly to meet current electrical and fire codes. The building inspector and fire marshal required a complete fire alarm system, and Alfano Electric performed the installation in accordance with those requirements. Mr. Tony Alfano further explained the fire alarm system design, panel installation, and programming, along with the scheduling of the fire department inspection.

Loss Solutions Group has reviewed the applicable codes governing the work performed. The following codes and standards are relevant to the scope of work presented:

- Per **NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code), Article 210, Section 210.8(B)**, all 125-volt through 250-volt receptacles in commercial kitchens and food preparation areas require ground-fault circuit-interrupter (GFCI) protection. This requirement applies to the twenty-two (22) dedicated GFCI receptacles, nine (9) single-pole 20A GFCI breakers, four (4) double-pole 20A GFCI breakers, and three (3) 20A GFCI deadfronts installed at the diner.

- Per **NFPA 72 (National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code)** and **NEC Article 760**, commercial occupancies require a complete fire alarm system including smoke detectors, heat detectors, manual pull stations, audible/visual notification appliances (strobes and beacons), a fire alarm control panel, and a remote annunciator. The fire alarm system installed at Charlie's Diner, consisting of three (3) beacons, five (5) strobes, four (4) door pull stations, seven (7) smoke detectors, three (3) heat detectors, one (1) alarm panel, one (1) remote annunciator, and one (1) StarLink cellular communicator, is consistent with these requirements.

- Per **NFPA 72, Section 10.18** and **Massachusetts 527 CMR 1.00**, fire alarm systems in commercial buildings must be monitored by an approved supervising station. The StarLink cellular communicator satisfies this monitoring requirement.

- Per **NFPA 70, Article 700** and **NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code)**, commercial buildings require emergency illumination along all means of egress, including illuminated exit signs and emergency lighting with a minimum 90-minute battery backup. The four (4) exit sign/emergency light combos (three (3) remote head capable and one (1) standard), seven (7) emergency lights, and three (3) remote heads for emergency egress lighting are consistent with these requirements.

- Per **527 CMR 31.00 (Massachusetts Carbon Monoxide Alarm Code)**, carbon monoxide detection is required in commercial buildings with fossil fuel burning equipment. The three (3) 120V CO detectors installed are consistent with this requirement.

- Per **NFPA 96 (Standard for Ventilation Control and Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations)**, commercial cooking exhaust hoods require service disconnects and the fire suppression system must be interconnected with gas shut-offs and electrical power to heat-producing equipment. The three (3) service disconnects for the exhaust hoods, two (2) gas shut-offs, one (1) gas reset, three (3) receptacles under the hoods to the Ansul system with contactors, and the two (2) three-pole contactors are consistent with these requirements.

- Per **NFPA 70, Article 600** and local building codes, exterior signage requires a dedicated disconnect on the roof, and a service receptacle is required for rooftop equipment servicing. The sign disconnect and service receptacle on the roof are consistent with these requirements.

- Per **Massachusetts 780 CMR (State Building Code)** and local fire department requirements, a key box (Knox Box) must be provided for fire department access. The key box installed at the diner is consistent with this requirement.

- Per **NFPA 72, Section 10.16**, two (2) modules are required to interface the fire alarm system with the existing Ansul fire suppression system, allowing the fire alarm panel to monitor the suppression system status. These modules are consistent with this requirement.

A total amount of $31,853.30 was presented.

Loss Solutions Group submits that the insured reportedly sustained a fire event on August 13, 2024, resulting in the need for a complete electrical and fire alarm system code upgrade to bring the restaurant into compliance for re-opening.

The equipment being installed is new and is required to meet current code standards. As such, there is no prior age or condition to report for the code upgrade components.

Loss Solutions Group was not asked to determine the cause of loss for this assignment. The code upgrade work was required as a result of the rebuilding process following this fire event.

Loss Solutions Group has reviewed the information provided, and we have discussed the scope of work with the vendor involved. ### ALFANO ELECTRIC LLC (413-636-3676) CODE UPDATES

Loss Solutions Group has reviewed the amount of $31,853.30 for electrical and fire alarm code upgrade work at the insured's restaurant.

Included in the cost is an amount for a complete fire alarm system (including three (3) beacons, five (5) strobes, four (4) door pull stations, seven (7) smoke detectors, three (3) heat detectors, two (2) Ansul modules, one (1) remote annunciator, one (1) alarm panel, and one (1) StarLink cellular communicator), a key box for fire department access, three (3) 120V CO detectors, twenty-two (22) dedicated GFCI receptacles, four (4) exit sign/emergency light combos, seven (7) emergency lights, three (3) remote heads for egress lighting, nine (9) single-pole and four (4) double-pole GFCI breakers, three (3) GFCI deadfronts, piping and wiring for gas shut-offs and Ansul system connections, two (2) three-pole contactors, a service receptacle and sign disconnect on the roof, three (3) service disconnects for exhaust hoods, and miscellaneous electrical materials.

These items are required per the electrical and fire codes cited above, including NFPA 70 (NEC) Articles 210, 600, 700, and 760; NFPA 72; NFPA 96; NFPA 101; Massachusetts 780 CMR; and 527 CMR. We submit that these components were required to bring the establishment into compliance for re-opening and the costs are fair and reasonable.

It should be noted that the vendor's invoice contained a calculation error on the exit sign/emergency light combo remote head capable line item. The invoice listed this item at $888.00, however the correct total for three (3) units at $156.00 each ($468.00 materials) plus four (4) labor hours at $140.00 per hour ($560.00 labor) should be $1,028.00. This represents a $140.00 difference that was confirmed by the vendor. The corrected total for the scope of work is $31,993.30.

Loss Solutions Group has reviewed the labor rate of $140.00 per hour charged by Alfano Electric LLC. We submit that this rate is fair and reasonable for a licensed electrician in the West Springfield, Massachusetts area performing code upgrade work.
